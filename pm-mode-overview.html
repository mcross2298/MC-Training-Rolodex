Executive Summary
Program Manager Mode — the owner's control layer
Program Manager Mode · The Big Picture

Run your app like a product — from your phone

Program Manager Mode is the owner-only control layer that sits on top of the live app. From a single toolbar you can rename and re-spec every exercise, program and split, restructure how they look, re-skin the whole app, spin up new content, group it into folders, and push it all to every user with one tap. Everything is paint-on-top: your originals are never rewritten, and nothing reaches users until you publish. This page is the full end-to-end — read it once and you'll know exactly what PM Mode can do.

1
The control room — module, hub & pill

Invisible to everyone but you. Enter from a dashboard module, work from a clean tools hub, and publish from a pill that follows you everywhere.

  • ✓Owner unlock — sign in with your owner account, then Face ID / Touch ID gates entry on your device. Normal users never see a trace of any of this.
  • ✓The “PM Mode” dashboard module — an owner-only card on your dashboard, and the single entry to every tool. Tap it to unlock and open the tools hub: a tidy sheet of every action — Edit Layout · Rename Center · ＋ Create · Find · Publish · Preview · History · Drafts · Client Roster · Export · Import · Guide · Discard · Lock.
  • ✓A persistent Publish pill — a compact 🛠️ PM · N edits · Publish pill sits at the top of every screen while you're unlocked. It's Publish-only, so you can push changes live from anywhere; everything else lives in the hub, one tap away from the dashboard module.
  • ✓Preview as user — flip to exactly what your users see (published only), with your in-progress edits hidden. Lock when you're done and the app is a normal app again.

Think of it as a hidden cockpit. The app stays a clean training app for everyone else; you tap into a control room layered on top of the very same screens — no clutter, just a small pill and a hub when you need it.

2
Rename & re-spec anything

The founding power of PM Mode: permanently re-label and re-program the content itself — for every user — without touching the original files. It's all paint-on-top, so it's fully reversible.

  • ✓The Rename Center — one screen to rename programs, splits, and badges. Give a program a new name, icon and description; relabel its splits; restyle the set-type badges — searchable, and it remembers the program you were last editing.
  • ✓Per-exercise editor — open any exercise card in PM Mode and rename it plus re-spec its sets, rest, tempo, and notes. A tier toggle lets you change it just for this split or globally across every page the exercise appears on — with a live indicator showing which tier supplies the visible name.
  • ✓Reset cleanly — clear an override and the card snaps back to the original, authored value. Your members' logs always key off the original identity, so renames never scramble history.
  • ✓Find — jump straight to any exercise from the PM bar to edit it, no scrolling.

This is the original heart of PM Mode: the program and exercise data you ship is yours to relabel and retune at any time, for everyone, and undo just as easily.

3
Bonus Workouts — zero-effort routing

Build a one-off workout and you don't have to file it anywhere. The app files it for you.

  • ✓Build it, skip the paperwork — in the Workout Builder, the “Assign to program” control is optional. Leave it on Bonus and you're done.
  • ✓It auto-collects — any unassigned workout is routed into a permanent Bonus Workouts card on the dashboard, newest first. No manual linking, ever.
  • ✓Reassign anytime — point a Bonus workout at a real program later and it quietly leaves the Bonus shelf. Change your mind freely.
4
Layout templates — swap the structure

Four core views, each with selectable structural styles. A swap re-flows the existing content — it never rewrites your programs or workouts.

Program Cards4 styles
Stack · Grid · Featured · Carousel. Reshape the dashboard's program grid from a clean vertical stack to a dense matrix, a hero-plus-grid, or a horizontal swipe.
Landing Pages3 styles
Hero · Split · Timeline. Present a program as a bold hero stack, a sticky-summary split view, or a vertical week-by-week timeline tracker.
Split Cards3 styles
Accordion · Tabbed · Week-Calendar. Collapsible day rows, top week tabs, or a 7-day calendar grid. (Accordion is the default for Strength & Supersets only — every other program defaults to tabbed.)
Workout Cards3 styles
List · Swipe · Superset-Grouped. A chronological list, one card-block at a time, or paired supersets bracketed as a single emphasized block.

Every style is additive and behind a toggle — your users see the shipped default until you publish a different one. Swapping never destroys content; it only changes how it's arranged.

5
The theme engine — re-skin the whole app

One ThemeConfig drives the app's chrome. Pick a vibe in a tap, or fine-tune each control.

  • ✓Preset palettes — one tap sets background, surface, accent and type together: Midnight Gold, Crimson Steel, Violet Haze, Deep Teal, Ember.
  • ✓Accent color — override the highlight used on active states, buttons, progress and PRs — or leave it Auto to follow the pinned program's color.
  • ✓Typography — switch between Clean Sans and High-Impact Athletic Bold.
  • ✓Density & Motion — tighten or relax spacing (Compact · Default · Spacious) and dial the animation level (Full · Subtle · Off, always honoring reduce-motion).

6
Edit Layout — change it live, in place

Tap Edit Layout on any screen and that screen drops into a draft state with a config sidebar.

  • ✓Context-aware — the sidebar offers the layout options for the view you're on (program cards on the dashboard, workout layout on a session, landing & day-list on a custom program) plus the global theme controls.
  • ✓Instant preview — every change repaints live, on the real screen, against real content. No mockups.
  • ✓Save & Publish or Discard — commit it live for everyone, or throw the draft away and snap back to the published look. Nothing leaks until you say so.
7
The “＋” Creator — make new things

A guided wizard for spinning up content, with a fixed, foolproof order.

  • ✓Pick a type — New Program · New Workout · New Card Template.
  • ✓Structure → Skin → Destination — choose the baseline layout, apply a theme skin (previews live), then hand off to the right builder for content. Your structure choice rides along onto the new item.
  • ✓Smart destination — a new workout left unassigned drops straight into Bonus Workouts; a new program gets its own landing layout from your structure pick.
8
Collections — folders for your workouts

Group standalone workouts however you think about them — by goal, body part, intensity, anything.

  • ✓Make a folder — name it, pick an icon and color on the Collections page. It's yours, synced across your devices.
  • ✓Tag on save — the Workout Builder has an “Add to collection” picker; a workout can be Bonus or program-assigned and live in a collection at the same time.
  • ✓Surfaces automatically — every non-empty collection becomes its own dashboard card that opens a clean, newest-first list.
9
Publish, drafts & safety — one tap, fully reversible

Every change — a rename, a layout swap, a new theme — flows through one working copy and one Publish, with guardrails the whole way.

  • ✓Paint-on-top — names, exercise specs, layouts and themes are overlays keyed to your originals. The authored content and your members' logs are never rewritten.
  • ✓One working copy, one Publish — edits stack locally as a draft with a live count. Publish pushes them live for everyone in about a minute; an Export / Import pair moves the same working copy as a file when you'd rather commit it offline or carry it between devices.
  • ✓Review before you ship — Publish shows a diff sheet of exactly what's changing. Discard wipes the draft; History lets you look back at past published states and restore one.
  • ✓Stage it — server-side Drafts let you park a working copy and load it later, and you can push naming changes To testers (a canary audience) first, then Promote to everyone when you're happy.
  • ✓Owner-locked end to end — writes are owner-only and enforced server-side, so only you can change what users see.

That's the whole machine — a hidden toolbar that lets you restructure, re-skin, create and group, all previewed live and shipped (or thrown away) with one tap. Nothing breaks, nothing leaks, and every change can be walked back.

10
Client Roster — suggest, don't remote-control

Assign a program and macro goals to a trainee who already has an account. It's coaching by suggestion, not by force.

  • ✓Add by email — the client needs to have signed into the app at least once already; ＋ Add client looks up their account by email (server-side — you can't browse the user list, and nobody's private data is ever exposed to you beyond their email/id).
  • ✓Assign a program + macro goals — tap Assign on any client to pick a program from the same list your dashboard uses, and set optional calorie/protein/fat/carb targets.
  • ✓Suggestion, not a push — the assignment lands on the client's own dashboard as a dismissible card. They tap Accept program / Apply goals themselves — nothing on their device changes until they do. There is no way for PM Mode to write into a client's own program pin or macro goals directly.
  • ✓Remove anytime — taking a client off your roster only removes your assignment record; their own account, logs, and data are never touched.

This is the one place PM Mode reaches past your own account — and it's built to only ever suggest, never overwrite. A client's data stays theirs to change, always with their own tap.
