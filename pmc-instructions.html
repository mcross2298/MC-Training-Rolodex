Tempo Ratios
4:0:1:0
4 sec negative, no pause, 1 sec lift, no pause
4:0:1:2
4 sec negative, no pause, 1 sec lift, 2 sec pause at top
1:0:1:2
Normal negative, 2 sec pause at bottom/top
1:2:1:2
2 sec pause at both bottom and top of movement
Types of Lifts
PYRAMID
15, 12, 10, 10 or 12, 10, 8, 8 — weight increases as reps decrease
HIGH SET / LOW REP
6×4 or 5×5 — strength focus, heavy load
LOW SET / LOW REP
3×5 or 4×6 — compound strength work
HIGH SET / HIGH REP
5×20 or 6×15 — volume and pump
LOW SET / HIGH REP
4×20 or 3×30 — metabolic stress
MINIMAL REST
5×8 with 20 sec break between sets
DROP SET
4×12 drop 6/8/10 or 12, 10, 8 drop 12/15 — strip weight, continue immediately
AMRAP
As many reps as possible — push until failure

How Each Workout Day Is Executed
⚡Option 5 — Daily Execution Pattern
Every workout follows this structure — one of each lift type per session:
1
Superset — two exercises back to back, no rest
2
Pyramid Lift — with drop set finishing (e.g. 12, 10, 8, 8 drop 12)
3
Low Rep Lift — heavy, 4–6 reps per set
4
Tempo / Ratio Lift — 4 sec negatives or 2 sec pauses
5
High Reps / High Sets — 5×20 or 6×15 pump finisher
Key Notes
⭐Exercises marked ** are alternatives
When you see **, you can substitute with the listed variation. Choose based on equipment availability or preference.
🚶Optional Walking Lunges
Leg days include an optional 100–200 walking lunges as a finisher. Highly recommended for glute/quad development.
🔄Week 1 vs Week 2
Each muscle group has a Week 1 and Week 2 variation. Week 2 increases intensity — heavier drops, longer negatives, or reverse exercise order.
